Cat

🐈
Domestic cat

The cat (Felis catus), also referred to as the domestic cat, is a small domesticated carnivorous mammal. It is the only domesticated species of the family Felidae. Valued by humans for companionship and its ability to kill vermin, the cat is now the most popular pet in the world.

Etymology

The origin of the English word cat, Old English catt, is thought to be the Late Latin word cattus, first used at the beginning of the 6th century.

It is commonly kept as a pet and working cat, but also ranges freely as a feral cat avoiding human contact.
