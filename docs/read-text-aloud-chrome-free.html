How to Make Chrome Read Text Out Loud for Free (No Account Needed)

Want Chrome to read text aloud while you browse? You don't need Speechify, a paid subscription, or even an account. This guide shows you how to get text-to-speech working in Chrome with a free, privacy-first solution.

Why You Need Text-to-Speech in Chrome

Reading text aloud helps you multitask, improve focus, and absorb information better. Whether you're dealing with long articles, research papers, or just want to listen while doing something else, text-to-speech is incredibly useful. The best part: you can get this feature for free without giving up your privacy or data.

Unlike services that track your reading habits or charge monthly fees, a privacy-focused TTS extension sends zero data to the cloud. Everything happens locally on your device.

How to Get Chrome to Read Text Aloud (Step-by-Step)

Step 1: Install a Free TTS Extension

The easiest way is to install the Voice Reader extension directly from the Chrome Web Store. No signup required.

  1. Open Chrome and go to the extension store
  2. Search for "Voice Reader"
  3. Click "Add to Chrome"
  4. Confirm the permissions prompt

Step 2: Select Text and Click Play

Now that you have the extension installed:

  1. Highlight any text on the webpage
  2. Click the Voice Reader icon in your toolbar
  3. The text will be read aloud using neural voices

Step 3: Customize Playback (Optional)

You can adjust playback speed, voice selection, and other settings through the extension's options. No configuration needed for basic use—it just works out of the box.

Accessibility and Reading Difficulties

Text-to-speech is especially valuable for people with dyslexia and reading difficulties. Hearing text while reading improves comprehension and reduces cognitive load. Voice Reader's word highlighting feature helps you follow along as each word is spoken, which is essential for accessibility.

How Does Offline TTS Work?

Voice Reader uses Piper VITS and WebAssembly technology to process speech entirely in your browser. Unlike cloud-based solutions, nothing leaves your device. No server farms processing your data, no privacy concerns, no loading delays.

Common Questions

Does Chrome have built-in text-to-speech?

Chrome has basic accessibility features, but they're not designed for everyday browsing. Voice Reader provides a much better experience with natural voices, playback controls, and word highlighting.

Will this slow down my browser?

No. The extension loads neural voices into your browser once, then uses them locally. There's minimal performance impact, and no network requests are made.

Can I use this offline?

Yes. Once the extension is installed, it works entirely offline. Perfect for flights, trains, or anywhere without internet.
